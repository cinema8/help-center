# How to use Google Fonts in Interactive Videos

## Step-by-Step Guide to Adding Custom Fonts to Your Cinema8 Projects

Cinema8 provides several built-in fonts for use in interactive videos. However, if your brand uses a specific font available on Google Fonts, you can integrate it into your player using a simple configuration process.

<Callout type="info">
This process allows you to apply Google Fonts globally to the player text elements for consistent brand styling.
</Callout>

<Steps>

### Step 1: Open the Customisation Menu

Log into your Cinema8 account and click **Customisation** from the left-hand sidebar.

![customisation panel](/screenshot/google-fonts/google-fonts-1.webp)

### Step 2: Navigate to Player Themes

Select **Player Themes** from the available customization options.

![player themes](/screenshot/google-fonts/google-fonts-2.webp)

### Step 3: Locate the Google Font URL Field

Choose a player configuration and scroll to find the **Google Font URL** input field.

![google font field](/screenshot/google-fonts/google-fonts-3.webp)

### Step 4: Select a Google Font

Visit [https://fonts.google.com](https://fonts.google.com). Choose your desired font and click the plus icon to add it. Then, copy the link from the bottom-left embed box.

![selecting font](/screenshot/google-fonts/google-fonts-4.webp)

### Step 5: Paste the Font URL in Cinema8

Return to Cinema8 and paste the Google Fonts URL into the **Google Font URL** field. Click **Save** to apply changes.

![paste url and save](/screenshot/google-fonts/google-fonts-5.webp)

</Steps>

<Callout type="important">
Once saved, the selected Google Font will be available for use in your interactive video elements such as buttons, text fields, and overlays.
</Callout>
