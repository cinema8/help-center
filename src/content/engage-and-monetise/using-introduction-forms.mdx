# Using Introduction Forms

<Callout type="info">
Start your videos strong—collect viewer information up front to personalize the journey and capture qualified leads.
</Callout>

Introduction forms are powerful interactive tools in Cinema8 that appear at the beginning of your video to collect key viewer details like names, emails, or preferences. Whether you’re generating leads or tailoring experiences, these forms set the stage for a high-impact interaction.

## How to Add an Introduction Form

<Steps>

### Step 1: Open Your Video in Creative Studio

- Log into your **Cinema8 Dashboard**.
- Choose your project and open it in **Creative Studio**.

---

### Step 2: Insert the Introduction Form Widget

- From the **Elements Panel**, drag and drop the **Introduction Form** widget onto the timeline at the start of the video.

---

### Step 3: Customize the Form Fields

- In the **Properties Panel**, select the input fields you want:
  - **Name**
  - **Email**
  - **Phone Number**
  - **Message**

---

### Step 4: Define Form Actions

- Set what happens when the form is submitted:
  - Redirect to a thank-you screen
  - Send an email confirmation
  - Store data to your CRM or marketing tool

---

### Step 5: Style the Form

- Match the form design to your brand by:
  - Setting fonts, colors, and button styles
  - Ensuring clear readability and responsive layout

---

### Step 6: Preview and Publish

- Test the form in the video preview
- Save your changes and publish when ready

</Steps>

## Best Practices for Introduction Forms

<Callout type="important">
A well-designed intro form sets the tone for your viewer’s experience—make it count.
</Callout>

- **Keep it short:** Ask only what’s necessary to avoid friction.
- **Explain the value:** Let viewers know what they’ll gain by filling out the form.
- **Make it attractive:** Use bold text and contrasting colors for better visibility.
- **Test before launch:** Always preview your video to ensure the form works properly.
